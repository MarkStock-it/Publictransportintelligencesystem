## 🎨 Design Guidelines for Slides

### Color Palette

- **Primary:** Navy blue (#1e3a8a)
- **Accent:** Teal (#14b8a6)
- **Text:** Dark gray (#1f2937)
- **Background:** White (#ffffff)
- **Highlights:** Soft yellow (#fef3c7)

### Typography

- **Headlines:** Bold, 48-60pt
- **Body:** Regular, 24-32pt  
- **Captions:** Light, 16-20pt
- **Font:** Inter, Roboto, or Poppins

### Layout

- **Clean and minimal** - Avoid clutter
- **High contrast** - Readable from distance
- **Visual hierarchy** - Guide eye flow
- **Consistent spacing** - Professional look
- **Icons over text** - Where possible

### Do's and Don'ts

✅ **Do:**
- Use high-quality images
- Limit text per slide
- Use bullet points
- Include visuals
- Practice transitions

❌ **Don't:**
- Overcrowd slides
- Use small fonts
- Read from slides
- Overuse animations
- Skip practice

## 🎤 Presentation Tips

### Before Presenting

1. **Practice 3-5 times** - Know your flow
2. **Time yourself** - Stay under limit
3. **Test equipment** - Projector, clicker, audio
4. **Backup plan** - USB, cloud, PDF version
5. **Water ready** - Stay hydrated

### During Presentation

1. **Face the judges** - Not the screen
2. **Make eye contact** - Connect with audience
3. **Speak clearly** - Pace yourself
4. **Use gestures** - Point at key elements
5. **Show enthusiasm** - You believe in this

### After Presentation

1. **Pause for questions** - Don't rush
2. **Listen fully** - Understand before answering
3. **Be honest** - "Great question, here's our thinking..."
4. **Show confidence** - Even if unsure
5. **Thank judges** - Professional close
